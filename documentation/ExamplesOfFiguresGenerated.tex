\section{\Large Example of figure obtained by the Collision Estimate}\label{sec:6.3.2}

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
%%%%%% data.pi
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
Figure \ref{fig:Collision} shows visually how to solve the equation in step7 by using the following:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\bar{X}'$ on the l.h.s. of equation in step 7 of the Collision Estimate,
\item $g(p)$ as the r.h.s. of equation in step 7 of the Collision Estimate, and
\item solution of $p$ with respect to given $X'$.
\end{enumerate}
As shown in ref.\cite{ImplementationNotes}, the r.h.s. of equation in step 7 of the Collision Estimate can be rewritten to as follows:
\begin{align}
g(p) & \equiv  p q^{-2} \left[ 1 + \frac{1}{2}(p^{-1} - q^{-1}) \right]F(q) - p q^{-1} \frac{1}{2}(p^{-1} - q^{-1}) \nonumber \\
&= 2 \left[ p(1 - p) + 1 \right] \nonumber \\
&= 2 \left[ -\left(p - \frac{1}{2}\right)^{2} + \frac{5}{4} \right] \label{eq:rhs632}
\end{align}
Here we used the following property:
\begin{align}
F(q) &= q \left( 2q^{2} + 2q + 1 \right) \label{eq:rewFin632final}
\end{align}
Now finding the solution of $p$ with respect to given $X'$, reduces to solving the following quadratic equation:
\begin{align}
\bar{X}' = 2 \left[ p(1 - p) + 1 \right].
\end{align}
In Figure \ref{fig:Collision}, we get the solution $p$ is 0.673833 for given $X' = 2.43956$.
